Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 31, 2026

Welcome to Pulse ("we", "our", or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and disclose information when you use our mobile application.

1. Information We Collect

We do not require you to create an account, and we do not directly collect or store personal identifying information such as your name, address, or phone number on our own servers. However, to provide a stable and optimized experience, our app uses third-party services that automatically collect certain data.

Information collected automatically:

  • Device Information: Device model, operating system version, unique device identifiers, and mobile network information.
  • Usage Data: Information about how you interact with our app (e.g., button clicks, time spent on screens, and feature usage).
  • Crash Reports: Stack traces and relevant device state logs when the application encounters an error or crashes.

2. Third-Party Services

To analyze app usage and maintain app stability, we utilize tools provided by Google Firebase. These services process data on our behalf in accordance with their own privacy protocols.

Firebase Analytics (Google LLC)

We use Firebase Analytics to understand how users interact with Pulse. This helps us improve our features and layout. Firebase Analytics may track events such as screen transitions and user engagement metrics.

To learn more about how Google handles this data, please visit: How Google uses data when you use our partners' sites or apps.

Firebase Crashlytics (Google LLC)

We use Firebase Crashlytics to receive detailed reports when the app crashes. It automatically collects stack traces and point-in-time device metadata to help us identify and fix bugs quickly. This data does not personally identify you.

3. How We Use the Information

We use the automatically collected data solely to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the functionality of Pulse.
  • Understand app performance and usage trends.
  • Diagnose and resolve technical bugs and crash events.

4. Data Retention and Deletion

Since we do not run our own database for your user profile, we do not store your physical habit logs on our servers—your habit data is stored locally on your device. Third-party data collected by Firebase is retained according to Firebase's default security and retention policies.

5. Account Deletion and Data Control

Pulse does not support or require user accounts. Because we do not collect or store personal data on our servers, there is no "account" to delete.

  • Local Data: All of your habit logs, timers, and progress are stored exclusively on your device. You can delete this data at any time by simply uninstalling the App.
  • Firebase Data: Any anonymous technical data (crash logs or analytics) collected by Firebase is linked to an anonymous installation ID, not a personal identity.

6. Your Choices and Opt-Outs

You can manage your data and privacy in the following ways:

  • Device Settings: You can reset your Advertising Identifier or limit tracking via your iOS/Android system settings.
  • App Deletion: Uninstalling Pulse is the most effective way to ensure no further diagnostic data is sent and all local habit records are wiped from your device.

7. Children's Privacy

Our application is not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last updated" date at the top.
